CRITICAL APPRAISAL (Evaluating Organizational Data Quality)

## Data Source Quality Test

**Repository:** FEVS 2024 (Federal Employee Viewpoint Survey)
**Conducted By:** U.S. Office of Personnel Management (OPM)

**Credibility Indicators:**
- **Official Source:** U.S. government agency (established 1979)
- **Large Sample:** 600,000+ federal employee responses
- **Professional Methodology:** 20+ years consistent methodology
- **Transparency:** Full documentation of methods, response rates, variables

**Trustworthiness Concerns:**
- **Context Mismatch:** Federal/public sector vs. our private organization
- **Scale Difference:** Includes agencies with 10,000+ employees (we have 50)
- **Self-Report Bias:** Survey responses (not objective performance data)
- **Response Rate:** Typical 40-50% response rate (non-response bias present)

**Relevance to Our Situation:**
- Engagement variables align with our X (Engagement/turnover)
- Recognition questions directly relevant to our M (Recognition)
- Turnover intent relevant to our Y (Engagement Increase)
- Federal context may differ from professional services
- Large organization patterns may not match our 50-employee size

**Overall Data Quality:** MEDIUM-HIGH
- High credibility source (OPM professional survey)
- Moderate context applicability (sector/size differences)
- Use as benchmark (not direct equivalent)

## DATA (Raw Numbers from FEVS 2024)

**Dataset Description:**
- **Source:** 2024 FEVS Public Data File
- **Sample Size:** ~600,000 federal employee responses
- **Time Period:** Survey conducted Spring 2024, data released November 2024
- **Variables:** 100+ questions covering engagement, satisfaction, management, work environment
- **Coding:** 5-point Likert scale (1=Strongly Agree, 2=Agree, 3=Neither, 4=Disagree, 5=Strongly Disagree)

**Specific Variables Used:**

### Engagement Questions (X Variables):
1. **Q40:** "My training needs are assessed"
2. **Q12:** "I know what is expected of me on the job"
3. **Q42:** "My supervisor supports my need to balance work and other life issues"
4. **Q69:** "Considering everything, how satisfied are you with your job?"

7. **Q14:** "My training needs are assessed"

### Outcome Variable (Y):

### Organizational Context Variables:
- **DAGENCYSZ:** Organization size category
- **DSUPER:** Supervisory status

**Raw Data Snapshot:**
- 600,000+ rows (individual responses)
- Values range 1-5 for engagement questions
- Binary 0/1 for turnover intent

## Barrier 1: Inaccurate Data

**Does this barrier apply?** ‚ö†Ô∏è PARTIALLY

**Data Collection Process (FEVS):**
- OPM administers validated survey instrument ‚úì
- Direct download from OPM public data file ‚úì
- Combined multiple engagement questions (standard practice) ‚úì

**Potential Inaccuracies:**
- Self-report bias (perceptions vs. objective measures)
- Non-response bias (40-50% response rate)
- Social desirability effects

**How we addressed:** Large sample (600K) reduces random error; focused on patterns not precise estimates

**Severity:** MODERATE - Self-report and non-response create uncertainty

**Confidence Impact:** MODERATE NEGATIVE

## Barrier 5: Measurement Error

**Does this barrier apply?** ‚ö†Ô∏è YES

**Measurement Precision:**
- Self-report survey (less precise than objective metrics)
- 5-point Likert scale (reasonable precision)
- High test-retest reliability (OPM validation studies)
- Different people interpret "Agree" differently

**Impact:** ¬±2-3 percentage point error margin (62% could be 59-65%)

**How we addressed:** Focus on meaningful gaps (4-5 points), don't over-interpret small differences

**Severity:** LOW-MODERATE - Standard survey error

**Confidence Impact:** SLIGHT NEGATIVE

---

## Barrier 6: Wide Confidence Intervals

**Does this barrier apply?** ‚ùå NO

**Assessment:**
- Large sample size (600K) produces narrow confidence intervals
- Standard errors are small due to massive sample
- Statistical precision is HIGH

**Confidence Impact:** POSITIVE - Large sample increases precision

## Barrier 7: Timing Effects

**Does this barrier apply?** ‚ö†Ô∏è PARTIALLY

**Time Factors:**
- FEVS collected Spring 2024, released November 2024 (current)
- FEVS relatively stable 2019-2024 (65-68% range)

**Temporal Concerns:**
- Seasonality (Spring vs. year-end)
- Economic context may differ 2024 vs. 2025
- 6+ months lag time

**How we addressed:** FEVS shows stability over time, short 1-year timeframe limits drift

**Severity:** LOW - Recent data, stable benchmark

**Confidence Impact:** MINIMAL

## Barrier 8: Sampling Bias

**Does this barrier apply?** ‚ö†Ô∏è YES

**FEVS Sample:**
- 600,000 responses from 2+ million federal employees (~30% response)
- Voluntary participation (not random)
- Federal employees vs. professional services organization

**How we addressed:**
- Acknowledged sector differences
- Used as benchmark, not direct comparison
- Will triangulate with other evidence sources

**Severity:** MODERATE - Sector difference is real

**Confidence Impact:** MODERATE NEGATIVE

---

## Barrier 9: Confounding Variables

**Does this barrier apply?** ‚úÖ YES

**Confounding Variables in FEVS:**
- Job type (clerical, professional, technical roles)
- Agency mission (NASA vs. IRS different cultures)
- Geographic location (DC vs. rural field offices)
- Pay grade (GS-5 vs. GS-15 different experiences)
- Union membership

**Impact:** Hard to isolate "engagement" from "federal employment effects"

**How we addressed:** Filtered to mid-size organizations (controls for some size effects), acknowledged confounds

**Severity:** MODERATE-HIGH - Multiple confounds exist

**Confidence Impact:** MODERATE NEGATIVE

---

## Barrier 10: Interpretation Bias

**Does this barrier apply?** ‚úÖ STRONGLY

**Correlation Found:**
- FEVS shows supervisor patterns correlate with engagement
- FEVS shows engagement/turnover correlation (18.4% intent matches 18% actual)
- Does NOT prove engagement causes lower turnover

**Alternative Explanations:**
- Reverse causation (high turnover ‚Üí low engagement)
- Third variable (bad economy ‚Üí both low engagement AND people stay)
- Selection effect (engaged people less likely to leave)

**How we addressed:** Labeled as "evidence for hypothesis" not "proof," will supplement with scientific evidence

**Severity:** HIGH - Critical limitation

**Confidence Impact:** MAJOR NEGATIVE - Reduces confidence in Y outcome prediction
